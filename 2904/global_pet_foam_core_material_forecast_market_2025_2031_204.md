</p><p>Polyethylene-Terephthalate (PET) foam core material is a lightweight, high-performance thermoplastic used across various industries for structural applications. Derived from recycled PET plastics, this material is favored for its strength-to-weight ratio, excellent temperature resistance, and recyclability. PET foam is widely used as a core material in sandwich composite structures due to its durability, environmental benefits, and cost-effectiveness.</p><p>
</p><p>It is commonly utilized in applications such as wind turbine blades, marine components, automotive parts, and building panels. PET foam is available in various densities and thicknesses, allowing manufacturers to choose specifications that suit their specific design requirements. The material's excellent mechanical properties, combined with ease of processing, make it an ideal solution for manufacturers seeking lightweight and sustainable alternatives to traditional core materials such as PVC or polyurethane foams.</p><p>
