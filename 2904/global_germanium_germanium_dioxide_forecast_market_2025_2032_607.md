</p><p><strong>Germanium and Germanium Dioxide are chemical substances that play an essential role in the semiconductor, fiber optics, and solar power industries. Germanium (Ge) is a lustrous, hard-brittle, grayish-white metalloid with the atomic number 32, belonging to the carbon group. It shares chemical characteristics with silicon and tin and serves as a semiconductor material in electronics. On the other hand, Germanium Dioxide (GeO2) is a white powder derived from germanium used primarily in the production of optical fibers and infrared optics. The high refractive index and excellent transmission properties make it indispensable in high-performance optical applications.</strong></p><p>
