</p><p>Hollow Core Bragg Layered Fiber (HCBF) is an advanced optical fiber technology that utilizes a photonic bandgap structure to guide light through an air or vacuum core surrounded by multiple dielectric layers. These layers act as Bragg reflectors, confining light within the hollow core by reflecting specific wavelengths based on constructive interference. Unlike conventional optical fibers that rely on total internal reflection in a solid core, HCBF employs a periodic structure that manipulates light at a fundamental level, allowing for low-loss transmission over broader wavelengths, including visible, infrared, and terahertz (THz) bands.</p><p>
</p><p>HCBF is primarily used in applications that demand high precision and low dispersion, such as wavelength filtering, laser output tuning, and high-energy pulse transmission. This fiber is especially suited for transmitting femtosecond laser pulses due to its ability to handle ultra-short durations and high peak powers without damage. The use of various materials, such as polymers and plastics, further enhances HCBF's adaptability across different spectral regions. Its lightweight, flexibility, and ability to minimize nonlinear effects make it a preferred choice in modern optical communication and advanced photonic applications.</p><p>
