<strong>Definition</strong></p><p>
</p><p><strong>Ferrous Sulfate Heptahydrate</strong>, commonly known as <strong>iron(II) sulfate heptahydrate</strong> (FeSOâ·7HâO), is a chemical compound widely used in various industries, primarily for its iron content. This crystalline solid, with a greenish color, is the heptahydrate form of ferrous sulfate and contains seven molecules of water of crystallization. It is typically produced by reacting sulfuric acid with iron ores, such as pyrite, and is an essential ingredient in several processes, including water treatment, the production of fertilizers, and as an iron supplement in the pharmaceutical and nutraceutical industries.</p><p>
</p><p>Ferrous Sulfate Heptahydrate is also used in <strong>agriculture</strong> for <strong>fertilizer formulations</strong> to correct iron deficiencies in plants. Moreover, it finds applications in <strong>water purification</strong> as a coagulating agent and in the <strong>production of pigments</strong> and <strong>dyes</strong>. It is a key ingredient in <strong>animal feed</strong> additives, and <strong>industrial chemicals</strong>, and plays an important role in environmental engineering due to its coagulation and flocculation properties in wastewater treatment processes.</p><p>
